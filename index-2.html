BIOINFORMATICS

Bioinformatics is an interdisciplinary field that merges biology with computational science to analyze and interpret biological data. It encompasses sequence analysis, structural biology, genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metagenomics, and systems biology. By developing and applying computational methods and tools, bioinformatics enables researchers to decipher genetic information, study molecular processes, and understand biological systems at various levels of complexity. With applications in medicine, agriculture, biotechnology, and environmental science, bioinformatics plays a pivotal role in advancing our understanding of life sciences and driving discoveries with profound implications for human health and the environment.
